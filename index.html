Why should you even learn this stuff?

Well, it's no secret that it is cheaper to build your own machine instead of buying a pre-built one.
There are also a lot of other benefits other than saving a buck.

  • Quality Control: Choose quality components. (Note: Good cooling is often lacking in pre-built PCs!)
  • Customization: Tailor the specs to fit your needs.
  • Upgradability: Easier to upgrade individual components in the future, rather than buying a brand new one.
  • Cost-Efficiency: This is where quality control comes in! You can cheap out here and there, without affecting performance.

The Essentials

Building a PC may seem like a hassle, but it's really simple when you break it down to its essential components

  • Processor (CPU): The "brain" of the computer.
  • Motherboard: The main circuit board that connects all components.
  • Graphics Card (GPU): Necessary for gaming and video editing.
  • RAM: Random Access Memory for temporary data storage while the computer is working.
  • Storage: Hard Drive (HDD) or Solid State Drive (SSD) to store permanent data.
  • Power Supply Unit (PSU): Provides electricity to the PC.
