Why should you even learn this stuff?

It's no secret that building your own PC can save you money. But the benefits don't stop at your wallet.

  • Quality: Choose quality components: (Note: Good cooling is often lacking in pre-built PCs!).
  • Customization: Tailor the PC to fit your specific needs, at the same time you can give it the looks you always dreamt about.
  • Upgradeability: Easier to upgrade individual components in the future, rather than buying a brand new one.
  • Cost-efficiency: This is where it gets interesting! You can cheap out here and there, without affecting the performance you need.

The Essentials

Building a PC may seem like a hassle, but it's really simple when you break it down to its essential components!

  • Processor (CPU): The "brain" of the computer.
  • Motherboard: The main circuit board that connects all components.
  • Graphics Card (GPU): Necessary for gaming and video editing.
  • RAM: Random Access Memory for temporary data storage while the computer is working.
  • Hard Disk Drives and SSD: SSD is the way to go these days, fast reliable storage.
  • Power Supply Unit (PSU): Provides electricity to the PC, very important not to cheap out on.
