Why should you even learn this stuff?

It's no secret that building your own PC can save you money. But the benefits don't stop at your wallet.

  • Choose quality components. (Note: Good cooling is often lacking in pre-built PCs!)
  • Tailor the PC to fit your specific needs, at the same time you can give it the looks you always dreamt about
  • Easier to upgrade individual components in the future, rather than buying a brand new one.
  • This is where quality control comes in! You can cheap out here and there, without affecting the performance you need.

The Essentials

Building a PC may seem like a hassle, but it's really simple when you break it down to its essential components!

  • Processor (CPU): The "brain" of the computer.
  • Motherboard: The main circuit board that connects all components.
  • Graphics Card (GPU): Necessary for gaming and video editing.
  • RAM: Random Access Memory for temporary data storage while the computer is working.
  • Storage: Hard Drive (HDD) or Solid State Drive (SSD) to store permanent data.
  • Power Supply Unit (PSU): Provides electricity to the PC.

Fun Trivia about computing

  • Intel introduced the first microprocessor in 1974.

  • The famous name "Windows" was originally called "Interface Manager"
